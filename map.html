Vía de la Plata and French Way

The Vía de la Plata, or Camino Mozárabe, is the longest Camino route, stretching over 1000 km from southern to northern Spain. Originally a Roman trade road linking the north and south of the country, it evolved into a pilgrimage route during Moorish rule, used by North African Christians on their way to Santiago de Compostela.

The French Way (Camino frances), is the most popular route of El Camino de Santiago. It runs from Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port on the French side of the Pyrenees to Roncesvalles on the Spanish side and then another 780 km on to Santiago de Compostela.
